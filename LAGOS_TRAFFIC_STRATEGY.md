## üá≥üá¨ 1. Solving the Lagos Traffic Feedback Loop

Lagos traffic is a "nuanced cocktail" of interconnected issues. ROADvision_Lagos addresses these through:

### A. The "Gridlock-Pothole-Rain" Feedback Loop
- **Problem**: Rainfall leads to erosion, which creates potholes. Potholes on arterial routes (e.g., Third Mainland Bridge) cause massive gridlock. Gridlock prevents maintenance vehicles from reaching the site.
- **Solution**: **Flood-Pothole Correlation Service**. By predicting erosion hotspots **before** the rain hits, maintenance can clear drains and reinforce asphalt proactively.

### B. LASTMA Integration (Sentinel Command Link)
- **Problem**: Lack of real-time communication between infrastructure state and traffic control.
- **Solution**: The **Sentinel Hub** provides **LASTMA** (Lagos State Traffic Management Authority) with immediate diversion suggestions when a "Critical" hazard is detected on high-volume routes.

### C. Market-Day Adaptive Analysis
- **Problem**: Lagos market days (Mushin, Oshodi, etc.) create unique traffic densities that traditional AI models fail to parse.
- **Solution**: **Adaptive ROI**. ROADvision_Lagos self-adjusts its detection regions to focus on the road surface while filtering out high-density pedestrian and market-stall distractions.

### I. Lagos Satellite Sentinel (Macro-Scale)
- **Objective**: Identifying road failure in inner-city streets (Mushin, Ajegunle) that are too narrow for mobile scan vehicles.
- **Outcome**: A city-wide health map that identifies "Infrastructure Blind Spots."

### II. Contractor Audit & Verification Portal
- **Objective**: Transparent governance of municipal maintenance contracts.
- **Outcome**: AI-verified "Before/After" reports that ensure pothole repairs meet quality standards before funds are released.

### III. Maintenance Urgency Score (MUS)
- **Objective**: Prioritizing limited government resources.
- **Outcome**: A 0-100 score that ranks road segments based on detection density, severity, and arterial importance.
